Startup Profile — Saveria
Ground truth for mentor matching · Extracted from deal memo, pitch deck, and Philippe + Jens notetakers

Sector & Stage

FinTech / AccountingTech / Vertical B2B SaaS · Pre-Seed

Geography

France (primary) → Germany via DATEV (second market) → EU

Business Model

Two-sided B2B SaaS: per-seat to accounting firms (€25/seat paid beta launching mid-May) + per-client-file pricing (€6→€3 at volume). 50% discount locked in for 2 years for early adopters.

Problem

Accounting firms operate across fragmented tools (banking, e-invoicing, payroll, DMS) with no intelligence layer. Structural shortage: ~21,000 accountants for 3.5M French SMBs — 50% of SMBs have no accountant; the rest receive minimal proactive advisory.

Solution & Evidence

AI-powered intelligence layer connecting to the full accounting stack. Human-in-the-loop by design: certified accountants validate every recommendation, keeping compliance with French fiscal regulation. Alpha launching imminently (free search/recommendation); paid beta mid-May 2026.

Target Customer

B2B: accounting firms (experts-comptables) — 6 design partners signed, strong inbound pipeline. Indirect: SMBs via their accountants.

Traction

Advanced prototype + 6 design partner firms co-constructing the product. Qonto integration pipeline (~25% of new French company creators). Pre-revenue — verbal & email commitments only; no LOIs yet.

Team

Arthur Bouhanna (CEO, ex-Qonto Head of Paid Acquisition: scaled acquisition from 5k to 650k SMBs across Europe, 8 years) · Benjamin Corsini (COO, ex-Wino co-founder & CRO, acquired by ISAGRI, 25% market share) · Antoine Moreaux (CTO, ex-Specify co-founder & CTO, raised €5M, scaled to Carrefour & Epic Games)

Key Bottlenecks for Mentoring
Identified from Jens notetaker (primary source), Philippe call, and deal memo
  • Pricing still untested — pre-revenue with no LOIs, only verbal commitments; paid beta launches mid-May at €25/seat with 50% discount; risk of mispricing or leaving money on the table
  • Germany expansion scheduled as second market — DATEV integration required; entering pre-PMF in France is a sequencing risk
  • Beta-to-paid conversion is the classic early-B2B failure mode — 6 design partners engaged but no paying customers yet
  • Two distinct product use cases validated (upsell surfacing vs per-collaborator profitability) — roadmap prioritization not settled
  • Crowded competitive landscape (Pennylane, Visma, DATEV, Spendesk, Finway, Moss) — "family doctor" narrative is a start but needs sharpening
  • Business model risk — Antoine's prior startup Specify failed explicitly because "ils n'ont jamais trouvé un vrai business model, très géré par des gens qui ont du produit beaucoup plus que du business"
  • GTM from zero — Arthur's acquisition experience is from a scaled Qonto, not a pre-seed cold start

Mentoring Areas & KPIs
5 selected areas · Directly tied to Saveria's bottlenecks · Trackable over 6-month program
Pricing Strategy
Objective: Validate and iterate pricing architecture through the paid beta launch — test willingness-to-pay across firm sizes, optimize the per-seat + per-client-file structure, and avoid leaving money on the table
KPIAverage Contract Value (ACV) per accounting firm — target €3k+ ACV by month 6, with ≥2 packaging experiments completed
Explicit from Jens notetaker (Saveria sourcing notetakers, Apr 2026), point #1: "Saveria's pricing model is still untested in the market… pre-revenue with no LOIs — only verbal and email commitments from beta users. The paid beta launches mid-May at €25/seat, with a 50% discount locked in for 2 years for early adopters. There's a real risk of mispricing or leaving money on the table, especially as they bridge from free alpha to paid product in a matter of weeks."
International Scaling
Objective: Sequence the Germany entry properly (via DATEV integration) without breaking French PMF focus; de-risk the DACH accounting market complexity
KPIGermany-readiness milestones: DATEV integration scoped + 5 qualified German firm conversations + 1 design partner by month 6
Explicit from Jens notetaker, point #2: "Germany is explicitly the planned second market after France, but it introduces significant complexity. A DATEV integration is required — Arthur has prior experience integrating with DATEV through Qonto, and the team claims some local accounting firm connections across markets. However, entering Germany pre-product-market-fit in France is a strategic risk."
Go-to-Market
Objective: Convert 6 engaged design partners into paying customers — structure the free-to-paid motion, set clear success metrics, and build a repeatable commercial playbook for accounting firms
KPI% MRR progression month-over-month — target 5 paying firms + €3k+ MRR by month 3, €15k+ MRR by month 6
Explicit from Jens notetaker, point #3: "Six design partners are signed, with strong inbound interest from other firms wanting beta access. The alpha (search/recommendation feature) launches imminently as a free tool, with the paid beta following in mid-May. The gap between 'engaged design partner' and 'committed paying customer' is where many early-stage B2B products stall." Reinforced by Philippe call (Apr 9): Arthur acknowledged his acquisition playbook at Qonto was at scale with "un budget immense… complètement démesuré" — he has not yet built zero-to-one commercial traction.
Product Development
Objective: Settle the roadmap across the two validated use cases — upsell/mission opportunity surfacing (universal) vs per-collaborator/per-file profitability (larger firms) — and install a disciplined prioritization process to prevent feature sprawl
KPI% of roadmap milestones delivered vs. plan — target 80% with a sharpened ICP (firm-size band) locked by month 3
Explicit from Jens notetaker, point #4: "Customer interviews validated two separate pain points: (1) surfacing upsell/mission opportunities within existing client portfolios (described as working 'almost universally') and (2) measuring profitability per collaborator/client file (more relevant for larger firms). These serve different segments and have different levels of universality. Choosing where to go deep first — and how to sequence the roadmap — will shape ICP, messaging, and resource allocation." Team risk: Antoine's prior company Specify failed because "très géré par des gens qui ont du produit beaucoup plus que du business" — business-disciplined product prioritization is exactly the pattern to reverse.
Partnerships
Objective: Structure and accelerate the Qonto distribution partnership (~25% of new French company creators) and sharpen competitive positioning against Pennylane / Visma / DATEV / Spendesk / Finway / Moss
KPI# of active strategic partnerships (Qonto signed + 1–2 additional channel partners) by month 6
Explicit from deal memo (Saveria_Memo.pdf): "Setting up an early integration and partnership with Qonto, providing a channel to address approximately 25% of new company creators in France." Reinforced by Jens notetaker, point #5: "The notes reference a wide range of potential acquirers and adjacent competitors: Penny Lane, Visma, DATEV, banks, SpendDesk, Finway, and Moss. This suggests the team is aware of a crowded landscape but may not yet have a sharpened positioning narrative against these players — particularly as some (Penny Lane, Visma) sit close to the accounting workflow."
